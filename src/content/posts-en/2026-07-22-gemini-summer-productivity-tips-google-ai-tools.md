Google has published a piece with 13 ideas for how college students can make good use of Google's AI tools over summer break. Organized around three themes — applying for a fall internship, trying new things, and studying smarter — it's a usage guide for existing Gemini-related tools rather than a new feature announcement.

## Details

- **Apply for a fall internship**:
  - Career Dreamer: input your experiences, skills, and interests to explore possible job opportunities
  - AI Mode's Canvas: build a dashboard to track internship deadlines and requirements
  - Gemini Notebook: upload career materials to refine your resume and identify your key strengths
  - Gemini Live: practice interviews and get real-time feedback on delivery, pacing, and tone
- **Try new things**:
  - YouTube Learning: access educational videos with summaries and quiz generation
  - Search Live: get expert guidance in real time through your camera
  - Gemini video analysis: get feedback on sports technique from recorded video clips
  - Google Lens: identify plants, landmarks, and local history from a photo
  - Android screen time tools: maintain healthy digital habits with reminders and bedtime scheduling
- **Study smarter**:
  - Gemini Study Notebooks: prepare for standardized tests like the GRE with diagnostic quizzes
  - AI Mode's Canvas: generate a custom study guide for a given subject
  - Gemini Notebook's Audio Overview: turn notes into a podcast-style format for studying on the go
  - Gemini Notebook: turn uploaded materials into study guides, quizzes, and interactive visualizations

## How to try it

- All of these tools are available with a Google account through the Gemini app, Google Search's AI Mode, YouTube, Google Lens, and Android's built-in features
- See Google's official blog post for further details on usage and language/region availability
