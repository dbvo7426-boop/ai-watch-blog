OpenAI has natively integrated its latest image generation model, DALL-E 3, into ChatGPT Plus and Enterprise. Users simply type a short request and ChatGPT automatically rewrites it into a detailed image-generation prompt before passing it to DALL-E 3 — a major shift that lets users get images close to their intent without needing prompt-engineering expertise.

## Details

- **Integration approach**: Designed so users can request images through natural conversation with ChatGPT, with no prompt engineering knowledge required
- **Quality improvements**: Compared to DALL-E 2, the model follows long, detailed prompts more faithfully and can render more legible text within images
- **Rollout**: Initially made available to ChatGPT Plus and Enterprise users
- **Safety measures**: OpenAI said it conducted red-teaming and built classifiers to assess real-world misuse risks before release

## What happened next

Following this ChatGPT integration, DALL-E 3 was also released as an API at OpenAI's first developer conference, DevDay, that November, letting developers use it directly without going through ChatGPT. This conversational approach to image generation went on to become one of the standard interfaces across image-generation AI more broadly.
