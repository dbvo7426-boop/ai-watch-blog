OpenAI removed the invite-only waitlist for DALL-E 2, allowing anyone to start using it immediately after signing up. About two months after expanding to an invite-based beta that July, the company moved to a wait-free general release.

## Details

- **Change in access model**: No more waitlist registration or waiting in line — users could start right after signing up
- **Usage stats**: At this point, more than 1.5 million users were generating over 2 million images a day, OpenAI reported
- **Community size**: More than 100,000 users were sharing their work and feedback in the Discord community
- **Pricing structure**: The system carried over from the invite-only era, granting new users a set number of free credits usable for generating, editing, and creating variations of images

## What happened next

Removing the waitlist caused DALL-E 2's user base to expand rapidly, and AI-generated images became a common sight on social media. In November of that year, an API was also released, letting developers embed DALL-E 2 into their own apps and accelerating its commercial rollout.
