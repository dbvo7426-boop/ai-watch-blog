Genspark announced it had raised $100M in a Series A funding round, alongside the official launch of its iOS app. The company positioned it as "the world's first AI search + browser + agent super app," saying it was refined internally through 52 iterations.

## Details

- **Funding**: Raised $100M in a Series A round (details on investors and post-money valuation were not disclosed)
- **What's in the iOS app**: Combines three features — AI search powered by a Mixture-of-Agents approach, a lightweight browser with built-in ad blocking, and an autonomous AI agent that handles chat, image generation, deep research, and fact-checking
- **Availability**: At launch, full functionality was iOS-only, with an Android version described as coming "soon"
- **Positioning**: A turning point where Genspark, which began as a search engine, expanded its scope into a "super app" integrating browser and agent capabilities

## What happened next

A month after this announcement, in April 2025, Genspark launched its autonomous "Super Agent," pivoting from a search engine into a full-fledged AI agent platform. That November, the company raised a $275M Series B and achieved unicorn status, continuing its rapid growth.
