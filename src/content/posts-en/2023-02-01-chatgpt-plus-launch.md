On February 1, 2023, OpenAI launched "ChatGPT Plus," ChatGPT's first paid subscription plan, priced at $20 a month. Since the free version was often subject to capacity limits due to surging demand, the paid plan gave users seeking a more stable experience a new option. It initially rolled out to waitlisted users in the US.

## Details

- **Price**: $20/month. Initially offered only in the US, expanding beyond the country on February 10, 2023
- **What's included**: Guaranteed general access even during peak usage times, faster response speeds, and priority access to new features
- **Continuation of the free tier**: OpenAI stated that subscription revenue would help sustain free access, saying it wanted to "continue to offer free access to as many people as possible"
- **Future plans teased**: At the time of the announcement, OpenAI also mentioned plans for a ChatGPT API and was exploring lower-cost and enterprise plans

## What happened next

ChatGPT Plus went on to serve as the priority access channel for later flagship models like GPT-4, becoming a pillar of OpenAI's revenue. This monthly subscription model was later expanded into multiple pricing tiers — Pro, Team, and Enterprise — forming the prototype of ChatGPT's current revenue structure.
