Google announced its new model, "Gemini 2.5 Pro." A reasoning-focused model that improves accuracy by going through an internal "thinking" process before responding, it shot to the top of the AI model comparison site LMArena by a wide margin immediately after release, drawing significant attention.

## Details

- **Thinking model**: Comes standard with a "thinking" capability that reasons through complex problems step by step before producing an answer
- **Context window**: Supports up to 1 million tokens (with an expansion to 2 million tokens announced as forthcoming). Can process text, audio, images, video, and entire code repositories together
- **Benchmarks**: Took a commanding lead on LMArena. Scored 18.8% without tool use on the challenging "Humanity's Last Exam" benchmark
- **Coding performance**: Achieved 63.8% on SWE-Bench Verified using a custom agent setup, earning high praise from developers
- **Availability**: Made available immediately to Google AI Studio and Gemini Advanced users through the Gemini app. Rollout to Vertex AI was to follow, with pricing to be announced later

## What happened next

Gemini 2.5 Pro was positioned as the flagship of Google's "thinking model" lineup, receiving further updates at Google I/O that May, and became the core driver of reasoning improvements across the Gemini series. Its strong reputation in coding also led to broader integration with developer tools.
