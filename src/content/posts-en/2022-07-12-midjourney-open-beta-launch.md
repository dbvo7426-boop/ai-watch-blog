Midjourney announced it had ended its invite-only closed beta and moved to an open beta that anyone could join simply by joining its official Discord server. The text-to-image experience, until then limited to a select group of users, was suddenly thrown wide open to the public.

## Details

- **How to access it**: Simply join the official Discord server and type a prompt with the `/imagine` command to start generating images
- **Background on the transition**: Moved from the invite-only closed beta that had been running since around March 2022 to an open beta requiring no application
- **Format**: At the time it was offered only as a bot on Discord, with no dedicated website or app
- **Reaction**: Generated images spread rapidly across social media and Discord communities, making Midjourney — alongside DALL-E 2 and Stable Diffusion — one of the sparks that ignited the image generation AI boom

## What happened next

This shift to open beta marked a turning point after which Midjourney's user base grew explosively, paving the way for major model overhauls in V4, V5, and V6, as well as a web version outside of Discord. Its distinctive approach of using Discord as the primary interface remained a hallmark of Midjourney for more than two years afterward.
