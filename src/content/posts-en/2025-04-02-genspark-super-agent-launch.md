Genspark announced its autonomous AI assistant, "Genspark Super Agent." Billed as doing more than just responding to conversation, it claims to independently handle planning, phone calls, content creation, analysis, and automation — marking a major pivot for a company that started out as an AI search engine in June 2024.

## Details

- **Technical foundation**: Described as "the world's first Mixture-of-Agents system," combining multiple optimized models, proprietary tools, and in-house datasets to handle a wide range of tasks
- **Performance claims**: Touted speed comparable to ordinary search and chat, a major reduction in errors and hallucinations, and user control that lets output be tailored to requests
- **Use cases shown in the demo**: Showcased a wide range of use cases, including trip planning with phone reservations, turning videos into presentation slides, turning research into reports, content generation, reaching out to influencers, and shopping on the user's behalf
- **Call For Me**: Also introduced a speech-to-speech feature that uses OpenAI's Realtime API to have the AI actually make phone calls and hold conversations

## What happened next

With the launch of Super Agent, Genspark completed its shift from a search engine to an agent-based workspace. This direction continued to be reinforced afterward, evolving by April 2026 into "AI Workspace 4.0," an integrated platform that extends to automating desktop operations and Office integration.
