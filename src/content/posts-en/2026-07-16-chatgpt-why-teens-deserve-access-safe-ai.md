OpenAI has published an article outlining the age-appropriate protections, learning support features, and parental controls designed to help teenagers use ChatGPT safely. Noting that nearly 90% of teen users use ChatGPT weekly for learning purposes, the article argues that AI access should be safeguarded, not restricted.

## Details

- **Core philosophy**: Denying teens access to AI would be like telling a previous generation not to use the internet or search engines until they turned 18. The focus is on ensuring access while building in safety measures
- **Automatic protections via age prediction**: Rather than requiring mandatory age verification at account creation, stricter default settings are automatically applied when the system estimates, based on usage patterns, that a user is under 18
- **Enhanced protections**: Restricts content involving violence, self-harm, dangerous "viral challenges," unhealthy body image content, and inappropriate romantic or sexual roleplay. Notifications also encourage breaks during long usage sessions
- **Parental controls**: Parents linked to a teen's account can enable Study Mode by default for new chats, set quiet hours, disable voice features, and manage access to image generation. They can also receive notifications when high-risk situations arise, such as signs of self-harm or violent threats that violate policy
- **Learning support feature, "Study Mode"**: Co-developed with educators and learning science experts, it guides students toward solving problems through step-by-step questioning and reflection rather than giving direct answers. Early evaluations have shown positive effects on academic performance
- **Other learning tools**: Includes study prompts for summarizing topics, turning notes into study guides, and creating flashcards; interactive learning content for math and science (covering 250+ topics, used by 18 million people weekly); and pronunciation practice in 61+ languages
- **Four safety principles**: (1) prioritize teen protection even when it competes with other goals, (2) direct users toward real-world support during crises, (3) treat teens as teens, not adults, responding in an age-appropriate way, and (4) set clear expectations through clear communication
- **Collaboration with outside organizations**: OpenAI has joined the Family Online Safety Institute and works with child safety experts, the anti-bullying organization Moonshot, mental health professionals, educators, and government agencies to continuously validate the effectiveness of its safety measures

## How to try it

This article isn't a new product announcement but a summary of OpenAI's safety approach. Parents can link a teen's ChatGPT account to their own to access parental controls such as setting quiet hours, receiving notifications, and defaulting to Study Mode. Specific setup instructions are available in the ChatGPT Help Center and on the official site.
