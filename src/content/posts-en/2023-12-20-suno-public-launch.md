Suno, Inc., based in Cambridge, Massachusetts, has publicly launched the web app for its AI music generation service "Suno." It was made available as a Microsoft Copilot plugin at the same time, drawing attention as a service that lets anyone generate songs complete with lyrics from a text prompt.

## Details

- **Availability**: Offered both through its own web application and simultaneously as a Microsoft Copilot plugin
- **What it does**: Automatically generates songs — including vocals and instrumentation — from text prompts or lyrics
- **Founding team**: Developed by Suno, Inc., founded by Mikey Shulman, Georg Kucsko, Martin Camacho, and Keenan Freyberg
- **Market position**: Became one of the early flagship examples of opening up the music-generation AI genre to general users, following in the footsteps of image and video generation AI

## What happened next

Three months after the public launch, in March 2024, Suno released "v3," touting radio-quality song generation. That May, the company raised $125 million in funding, achieving rapid growth right from launch.
