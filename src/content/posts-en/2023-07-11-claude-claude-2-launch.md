On July 11, 2023, Anthropic announced its new model "Claude 2." The bigger change here was not the model's raw capabilities but how it was delivered: Claude, previously accessible only through the API or partner apps, was now available for the first time directly to general consumers via the beta of a dedicated website, "claude.ai" (initially limited to the US and UK).

## Details

- **claude.ai beta launch**: The first official chat interface that ordinary consumers could use simply by signing up — a turning point where Anthropic's models moved from being a "developer-facing API" to a "consumer-facing product"
- **Performance gains**: Scores rose from 73.0% to 76.5% on a simulated bar exam, from 56.0% to 71.2% on coding (HumanEval), and from 85.2% to 88.0% on math (GSM8k). On the GRE's reading and writing sections, it scored in the top 10%
- **100,000-token context window**: Could read up to 100,000 tokens in a single prompt, enough to process hundreds of pages or an entire book, and could also generate outputs spanning several thousand tokens
- **Safety**: Anthropic said internal red-team evaluations showed a "twofold" reduction in harmful output compared to the previous version
- **Pricing**: API pricing remained unchanged from Claude 1.3, with early integrations from companies like Jasper and Sourcegraph

## What happened next

The public launch of claude.ai marked a turning point in Anthropic's evolution from a developer-focused company into one serving consumer products as well. This direction fed into the "Claude 3" family in 2024, and the expansion of free and Pro subscription plans that followed.
