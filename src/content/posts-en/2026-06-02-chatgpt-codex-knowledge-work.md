On June 2, 2026, OpenAI published a report titled "The Next Era of Knowledge Work," announcing that weekly active users of its coding agent "Codex" had surpassed 5 million. Usage has grown more than sixfold since the desktop app launched in February 2026. While developers remain the largest user group, non-development "knowledge work" — such as writing reports and documents — now accounts for about a fifth of total usage and is growing more than three times faster than usage by developers.

## Details

- **Over 5 million weekly users**: Codex's weekly active users have grown more than sixfold since the desktop app launched in February 2026
- **Surge among knowledge workers**: Non-developer "knowledge workers" now make up roughly 20% of Codex's user base, growing more than three times faster than developer usage
- **Main use cases**: Beyond producing work deliverables like reports, spreadsheets, presentations, and contracts, usage is expanding into research, data analysis, workflow automation, and building simple tools that previously required an engineer's help
- **Drivers of growth**: In addition to the February 2026 desktop app launch, the rollout of GPT-5.5 in late April is cited as a factor that helped extend Codex's reach beyond developers
- **A shift in positioning**: OpenAI says it is repositioning Codex from a "coding-only tool" to "a productivity tool for everyone"

## How to try it

Codex is available in several forms: the "Codex CLI" in the terminal, desktop apps for macOS and Windows, an "IDE extension" for tools like VS Code, and cloud agents within ChatGPT. It's accessible on ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, Edu, and Enterprise plans, and is designed so that even non-engineers can bring it directly into everyday work like document creation and data analysis.
