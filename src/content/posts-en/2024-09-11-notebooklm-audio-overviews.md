Google announced a new feature for NotebookLM called "Audio Overviews." With a single click, it generates audio content in which two AI hosts discuss and explain the content of uploaded documents in a back-and-forth conversation. After launch, it became a huge topic on social media, with many describing it as feeling "just like a podcast."

## Details

- **How it works**: Simply add sources to a notebook and click "Generate," and two AI hosts summarize the material and explain connections between topics in a conversational format
- **Downloadable**: Generated audio can be downloaded to listen to on the go
- **Limitations at launch**: As an experimental feature, it had constraints — generation could take several minutes for larger notebooks, hosts spoke only in English, and real-time interruption was not possible
- **Disclaimer**: Google itself noted that "this reflects the uploaded sources and is not a comprehensive or objective view"

## What happened next

Audio Overviews became an instant hit, propelling NotebookLM from a little-known Google experiment into a flagship product. That December, alongside a UI overhaul, Google announced the ability to interrupt the audio and a paid tier, "NotebookLM Plus" — with this feature serving as the springboard for a much faster pace of development.
