A startup led by Flo Crivello publicly launched "Lindy.ai," a platform for building no-code AI assistants (referred to internally at Lindy as "Lindys"). The launch came after more than a year of development and six months of private beta testing, and the response to a demo video quickly drove a waitlist of roughly 70,000 people.

## Details

- **Founder's background**: Flo Crivello is a former Uber product manager and founder of Teamflow, a virtual office service that gained popularity during the pandemic. As Teamflow's momentum faded once the pandemic subsided, he decided in early 2023 to pivot the company entirely toward AI agents
- **Product goal**: Positioned as letting anyone build their own personal AI assistant — without writing code — to handle a wide range of tasks, from managing email and calendars to fielding customer inquiries. The pitch: "an AI agent platform anyone can use, like Zapier"
- **Road to launch**: After more than a year of development and six months of private beta testing, the company opened access to all users on launch day
- **Reaction**: A demo video drove the waitlist to roughly 70,000 people, drawing significant attention right from launch

## What happened next

Lindy continued expanding its feature set after launch, posting to Show HN in February 2024 to reach a broader developer audience. In July of that year, it rolled out a major overhaul, "Lindy 2.0," enabling advanced workflow building through a flow editor.
