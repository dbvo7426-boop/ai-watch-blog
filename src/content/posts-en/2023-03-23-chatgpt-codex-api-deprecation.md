On March 23, 2023, OpenAI discontinued API access to its original Codex-family models (code-davinci-002, code-davinci-001, code-cushman-002, and code-cushman-001), which had been offered as a free beta since 2021. The company cited the fact that its successor general-purpose models, GPT-3.5 and GPT-4, now outperformed Codex even at code generation tasks — but the astonishingly short three-day transition window between developer notice and shutdown drew criticism that it had "shaken confidence in OpenAI as a reliable platform."

## Details

- **Discontinued models**: Four models — code-davinci-002, code-davinci-001, code-cushman-002, and code-cushman-001 — were removed from the API
- **Recommended migration path**: OpenAI recommended migrating to GPT-3.5-Turbo or GPT-4, judging that general-purpose models capable of handling not just code but also documentation, error messages, and software design context outperformed a code-only model
- **Short notice**: Developers were given only about three days between notification and the actual shutdown, sparking strong backlash from those who had integrated the Codex API into existing products
- **A casualty of its free-beta status**: Codex had been offered as a free beta ever since its 2021 release, never having become a formal paid product — a status likely contributing to how readily it was discontinued on short notice
- **Partial revival later**: After the full shutdown, access was later partially restored for some researchers through the "OpenAI Researcher Access Program," in response to demand for research use

## What happened next

The abrupt end of the original Codex made clear that OpenAI was prioritizing investment in general-purpose large models over single-purpose specialized ones. The "Codex" brand disappeared entirely at this point — only to make a dramatic comeback roughly two years later, in 2025, as an entirely redesigned cloud-based coding agent called "OpenAI Codex."
