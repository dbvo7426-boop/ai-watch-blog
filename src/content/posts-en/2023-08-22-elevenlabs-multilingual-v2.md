ElevenLabs has officially exited beta and released "Eleven Multilingual v2," a speech synthesis model supporting around 30 languages. It's a foundation model designed to make it easier for media companies, game developers, publishers, and individual creators to localize content for audiences around the world.

## Details

- **Language coverage**: Supports around 30 languages, covering Europe, Asia, and the Middle East
- **Automatic language detection**: Automatically identifies the language of input text and generates speech with appropriate emotional expression
- **Voice consistency**: Preserves a speaker's distinctive voice quality and accent across languages, for both synthetic and cloned voices
- **Intended use cases**: Envisioned for multilingual audiobook production, game localization, and creators producing content for overseas audiences
- **A milestone**: Moving to a full release roughly seven months after the January 2023 beta launch, underscoring the platform's growing maturity

## What happened next

Eleven Multilingual v2 became positioned as the multilingual foundation underpinning later dubbing and voice-agent features. Five months later, in January 2024, ElevenLabs raised an $80 million Series B, growing into a unicorn company valued at $1.1 billion.
