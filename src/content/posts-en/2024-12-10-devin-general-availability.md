Cognition announced general availability (GA) for its AI software engineer "Devin," first unveiled in March 2024. After a year-long early access period, enterprise engineering teams can now use it starting at $500/month with no limit on the number of seats.

## Details

- **Pricing and offering**: Starts at $500/month with no seat limit, including Slack integration, an IDE extension, API access, and onboarding support
- **Recommended use cases**: Suggested starting with small frontend bug fixes and edge cases, drafting initial pull requests from backlog tasks, and targeted refactoring using the IDE extension
- **How it works**: Slack serves as the primary interface — tag and assign a task to Devin, and it works through the task, reporting back via message when done. Also integrates with GitHub and VS Code
- **Recommended practices**: Offered tips such as "assign tasks you already know how to do yourself," "give clear testing instructions," and "keep sessions under three hours"
- **Track record**: Highlighted real contributions to open source projects including Zod, the Google Go client, and LlamaIndex

## What happened next

After GA, Devin steadily expanded enterprise adoption. In April 2025, Cognition launched Devin 2.0 with a significantly revised pricing model, and in July it further expanded its product lineup through the acquisition of Windsurf.
